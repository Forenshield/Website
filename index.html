Digital forensics is becoming increasingly critical in investigating and resolving cyber incidents, where the integrity and accessibility of data play a vital role in uncovering the truth. However, the sheer complexity and volume of modern digital environments pose significant challenges for forensic professionals. The volatility of data, coupled with sophisticated threats, can impede the ability to capture and preserve crucial evidence.

Our forensic tool is designed to address these challenges head-on. It serves as an essential asset for digital investigators, ensuring the secure and efficient acquisition, analysis, and preservation of digital evidence. By providing a comprehensive suite of functionalities, our tool empowers users to safeguard the integrity of data across multiple platforms, perform in-depth analysis, and generate reports that withstand legal scrutiny. With continuous updates to counter emerging threats, our tool fosters a reliable and resilient digital forensic process, empowering organizations to maintain their security posture in the face of evolving cyber threats.

LITERATURE SURVEY

The ForenShield project leverages advanced research to address key challenges in digital forensics, particularly with the growing use of Internet of Things (IoT) devices. Traditional forensic tools often struggle to keep up with the massive volume and complexity of data generated by IoT networks. These devices are highly diverse in their functionality, communication protocols, and operating environments, making it difficult to collect and analyze digital evidence.

In the realm of criminal profiling, the use of machine learning (ML) has proven to be an effective method for identifying behavioral patterns from digital evidence. ForenShield incorporates Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, a type of ML model particularly useful for analyzing sequential data such as browsing history, communication logs, and user interactions. However, traditional ML models often lack transparency, which can be problematic in legal contexts where investigators need to explain how conclusions were reached. To address this, ForenShield implements explainable AI techniques that ensure the findings are not only accurate but also interpretable and legally defensible.

Additionally, the project enhances the efficiency of keyword searching in large forensic datasets by employing advanced artificial intelligence (AI) and natural language processing (NLP) algorithms. These AI-driven tools rapidly sift through vast amounts of data, quickly identifying critical evidence while reducing the time investigators spend on manual searches. This allows for faster, more accurate investigations, particularly in complex cases involving large datasets.

To ensure secure data management and evidence integrity, ForenShield incorporates decentralized storage technologies. One key feature is the use of the InterPlanetary File System (IPFS) for Chain of Custody (CoC). Traditional methods of tracking evidence, such as centralized servers, are vulnerable to tampering and breaches. ForenShield overcomes these limitations by using IPFS and JSON Web Tokens (JWTs) to securely store and track digital evidence. This ensures that evidence remains immutable, verifiable, and tamper-proof from collection to presentation in court, providing a robust solution for maintaining the integrity of digital evidence throughout the forensic process.

METHODOLOGY

The ForenShield project employs a structured approach to system development and testing, specifically designed to address the following challenges of digital forensic investigations in IoT environments.

  1. IoT Data Collection
  2. Machine Learning for Criminal Profiling
  3. AI-Driven Keyword Searching
  4. Chain of Custody (CoC)


IoT Data Collection is facilitated through customized hardware that securely gathers data from various IoT devices, even those with limited processing power. This data is transmitted using secure communication protocols and stored via decentralized storage using the InterPlanetary File System (IPFS). By avoiding centralized servers, IPFS ensures that data remains tamper-proof and protected from unauthorized access.

Machine Learning for Criminal Profiling is implemented using a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model. This model analyzes browser artifacts like cookies and history logs to detect behavioral anomalies, helping investigators identify suspicious activities. ForenShield enhances transparency by integrating explainable AI, ensuring investigators can interpret how conclusions are reached, a crucial aspect for legal defensibility.

For AI-Driven Keyword Searching, the system leverages natural language processing (NLP) to automate the identification of relevant evidence. This advanced AI-based search tool understands the context behind search terms, significantly reducing manual effort and accelerating the discovery of key information in large datasets.

In managing the Chain of Custody (CoC), ForenShield replaces traditional blockchain methods with JSON Web Tokens (JWTs) and JSON tokens. This lightweight solution ensures secure, cost-effective tracking of digital evidence from collection to court presentation, preserving evidence integrity.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The ForenShield project addresses one of the most significant challenges in modern digital forensics: effectively investigating and preserving evidence in Internet of Things (IoT) environments. IoT devices are often distributed across various locations, diverse in their functionality, and constrained by limited resources such as storage and processing power. These factors make traditional forensic tools inadequate for handling the unique demands of IoT investigations. Conventional tools are not equipped to manage proprietary protocols, volatile data, and the rapid, transient nature of IoT devices, leading to gaps in evidence collection, analysis, and preservation.

ForenShield provides a comprehensive solution by integrating cutting-edge technologies to overcome these challenges. One key innovation is in the Chain of Custody (CoC) process, which is vital for maintaining the legal admissibility of digital evidence. In forensic investigations, ensuring that evidence is securely tracked from acquisition through to court presentation is critical. While blockchain technology has been proposed as a solution for managing CoC, it comes with high costs and technical complexities that make it difficult to implement on a wide scale.

Instead, ForenShield introduces an innovative alternative using JSON Web Tokens (JWTs) and JSON tokens to manage the CoC. This method offers a lightweight, scalable, and cost-effective solution that ensures the integrity of digital evidence throughout its lifecycle further which allows for secure, real-time tracking of evidence without the expense and complexity of blockchain systems, making it an accessible and practical choice for organizations of all sizes.

In summary, ForenShield effectively addresses the growing challenges of digital forensics in IoT environments. By integrating advanced technologies such as machine learning (ML), artificial intelligence (AI), and modern CoC techniques, the platform provides a robust, scalable, and efficient solution. It not only tackles technical and legal hurdles but also ensures cost-efficiency, making it a game-changing tool in the evolving landscape of digital forensics.

Main Objective

To provide a low-cost, AI-powered cybercrime investigation platform accessible to a wider range of users, including small businesses, individual investigators, and law enforcement agencies in resource-constrained regions.


Sub Objective 1

Develop a dedicated in-house hardware solution for data acquisition.

Sub Objective 2

Develop a keyword search model that overcome problems of traditional model.

Sub Objective 3

Develop a behavior profiling model that integrate traditional behavior analysis into digital forensics.

Sub Objective 4

Ensure the integrity, security, and admissibility of digital evidence from collection to court presentation.

RESEARCH GAP

The research gaps in this project lie primarily in the limited practical tools and methodologies available for forensic investigations in IoT devices. Current forensic techniques often struggle with issues like data acquisition from diverse IoT environments, data volatility, and maintaining a robust chain of custody. Additionally, most approaches remain theoretical, and the field lacks a unified methodology for conducting investigations across different IoT ecosystems. There is also a gap in integrating advanced behavioral profiling with digital forensics, and in utilizing AI-powered systems for improving keyword searches and contextual understanding in evidence analysis.
